# Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) Basics

## What is an ETF?

An Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) is a type of investment fund that holds a collection of assets like stocks, bonds, or commodities. ETFs trade on stock exchanges just like individual stocks, providing investors with diversification and liquidity.

## Key Features

- **Diversification**: One ETF can hold hundreds of different securities
- **Liquidity**: Trade throughout the day like stocks
- **Low Costs**: Typically lower fees than mutual funds
- **Transparency**: Holdings are disclosed daily

## Types of ETFs

- **Stock ETFs**: Track stock indices (e.g., S&P 500, NASDAQ)
- **Bond ETFs**: Invest in various bonds
- **Sector ETFs**: Focus on specific industries
- **Commodity ETFs**: Track commodities like gold, oil
- **International ETFs**: Invest in foreign markets

## Advantages

- Instant diversification
- Lower expense ratios than mutual funds
- Tax efficiency
- Easy to buy and sell

## Considerations

- Still subject to market risk
- May have tracking errors
- Trading costs apply
- Some ETFs have low liquidity
