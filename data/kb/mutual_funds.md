# Mutual Funds Basics

## What are Mutual Funds?

Mutual funds are investment vehicles that pool money from multiple investors to purchase a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities. They're managed by professional fund managers.

## Key Features

- **Diversification**: Instant diversification across many securities
- **Professional Management**: Managed by investment professionals
- **Liquidity**: Can buy or sell shares daily
- **Variety**: Available for different investment goals

## Types of Mutual Funds

- **Stock Funds**: Invest primarily in stocks
- **Bond Funds**: Invest primarily in bonds
- **Balanced Funds**: Mix of stocks and bonds
- **Index Funds**: Track market indices
- **Sector Funds**: Focus on specific industries

## Costs

- **Expense Ratio**: Annual fee as percentage of assets
- **Load Funds**: Sales charges (front-end or back-end)
- **No-Load Funds**: No sales charges
- **12b-1 Fees**: Marketing and distribution fees

## Advantages

- Professional management
- Diversification
- Convenience
- Variety of options

## Disadvantages

- Management fees
- Less control over holdings
- Potential tax implications
- May underperform market

## vs ETFs

- Mutual funds: Trade once per day at NAV
- ETFs: Trade throughout day like stocks
- ETFs typically have lower fees
