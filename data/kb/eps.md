# Earnings Per Share (EPS)

## What is EPS?

Earnings Per Share (EPS) is a financial metric that represents the portion of a company's profit allocated to each outstanding share of common stock. It's a key indicator of a company's profitability.

## Formula

EPS = (Net Income - Preferred Dividends) รท Average Outstanding Shares

## Types of EPS

- **Basic EPS**: Uses actual shares outstanding
- **Diluted EPS**: Accounts for potential shares (options, warrants)
- **Trailing EPS**: Based on past 12 months
- **Forward EPS**: Based on projected earnings

## Interpretation

- **Higher EPS**: Generally better, indicates more profit per share
- **EPS Growth**: Increasing EPS over time is positive
- **Negative EPS**: Company is losing money

## Use in Valuation

- **P/E Ratio**: Price รท EPS
- **EPS Growth Rate**: Measures company growth
- **Earnings Quality**: Consistent EPS is preferred

## Limitations

- Can be manipulated through accounting
- Doesn't reflect cash flow
- Share buybacks can inflate EPS
- Industry comparisons needed

## Factors Affecting EPS

- Net income changes
- Share count changes (buybacks, dilution)
- Accounting methods
- One-time events
