# Return on Equity (ROE)

## What is ROE?

Return on Equity (ROE) is a financial metric that measures a company's profitability relative to shareholders' equity. It shows how effectively a company uses shareholders' money to generate profits.

## Formula

ROE = (Net Income ÷ Shareholders' Equity) × 100

## Interpretation

- **High ROE**: Company efficiently uses equity to generate profits
- **Low ROE**: Company may be inefficient or have too much equity
- **Industry Comparison**: Compare ROE within the same industry

## DuPont Analysis

ROE can be broken down into three components:
- **Profit Margin**: Net income ÷ Revenue
- **Asset Turnover**: Revenue ÷ Assets
- **Equity Multiplier**: Assets ÷ Equity

## Factors Affecting ROE

- Profitability
- Asset efficiency
- Financial leverage
- Industry characteristics

## Use Cases

- Evaluating management effectiveness
- Comparing companies
- Investment analysis
- Performance tracking

## Limitations

- Can be manipulated through share buybacks
- High ROE from leverage may indicate risk
- Industry-specific benchmarks vary
- Doesn't account for growth potential
