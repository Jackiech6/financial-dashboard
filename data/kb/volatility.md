# Volatility in Financial Markets

## What is Volatility?

Volatility measures how much a stock's price fluctuates over time. It's typically measured using standard deviation of returns and indicates the level of risk or uncertainty in an investment.

## Types of Volatility

- **Historical Volatility**: Based on past price movements
- **Implied Volatility**: Market's expectation of future volatility (from options)
- **Realized Volatility**: Actual volatility that occurred

## Measurement

- **Standard Deviation**: Most common measure
- **Beta**: Compares stock volatility to market
- **VIX Index**: Measures market volatility expectations

## Interpretation

- **High Volatility**: Large price swings, higher risk
- **Low Volatility**: Stable prices, lower risk
- **Volatility Clustering**: High volatility periods tend to cluster

## Factors Affecting Volatility

- Market sentiment
- Economic news
- Company-specific events
- Market liquidity
- Trading volume

## Use in Investing

- Risk assessment
- Portfolio diversification
- Options pricing
- Risk management strategies

## Volatility Index (VIX)

The VIX measures expected 30-day volatility of the S&P 500. Often called the "fear index" - higher VIX indicates more fear/uncertainty in markets.
